# Phishing Attack Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals in to revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. a common example is receiving an email or message that appears to be from a bank or a well-known company.

In a typical phishing scenario, an student might receive an email claiming that their bank account will be blocked unless they verify their details immediately. The message often includes a link to a fake website that looks identical to the official one. When the victim enters their login credentials or OTP, the attacker captures this information and gains access to their account.

Students and young users are common targets because they frequently use digital platforms and may not always verify the authenticity of messages. The consequences can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

Phishing attacks rely on urgency and fear to manipulate victims. Therefore, awareness and cautious behavior are essential to prevent such cybercrimes.
